How to Rank Higher on Google Maps (The 3 Pillars of Local Algorithm)

Written by: Marcus Vance (Local SEO Lead) Published: May 28, 2026 Read time: 10 min read

Securing a spot in Google's Local 3-Pack is the single most effective way to drive customer phone calls and bookings to your service business. Learn the three core pillars Google uses to rank maps profiles and how to optimize your listing.

When potential customers search for a service nearby—whether it's a spa in Denver, a plumber in Houston, or a pizzeria in Chicago—Google doesn't display standard website search results first. Instead, it places the Google Maps Local 3-Pack at the top of the page. Listings that appear in this box receive the vast majority of clicks, calls, and routing requests.

Google's local search algorithm uses a different set of rules than regular web SEO. It evaluates listings based on three core pillars: Relevance, Distance, and Prominence. Let's break down each pillar and explore how to optimize for them.

Pillar 1: Relevance

Relevance refers to how well a business listing matches what a user is searching for. If Google doesn't believe your profile matches the searcher's intent, you will not rank, regardless of how close you are to their location.

How to Optimize Relevance:

  • Primary Category Selection: This is the single most important ranking signal in your dashboard. If you run a nail salon, your primary category must be "Nail Salon", not "Beauty Salon" (use "Beauty Salon" as a secondary category instead).
  • Expand Services Menu: Add every specific treatment or service you provide to your service list. Write descriptive, keyword-rich summaries (e.g. write "Gel Pedicure: A long-lasting gel nail polish applied to toenails with callus removal" rather than just "Pedicure").
  • Local Keywords in Bio: Write a comprehensive business description explaining your specialties, history, and the exact city and suburbs you serve.

Pillar 2: Distance

Distance measures how far your physical storefront is from the user's location at the time of their search. If a user searches "plumber near me", Google places high weight on profiles closest to the searcher's GPS coordinates.

You cannot change your physical location, but you can optimize how Google understands your service coverage.

How to Optimize Distance:

  • Define Service Areas: For service area businesses (like plumbers or movers who travel to customers), specify your target coverage zip codes or cities. Do not set a radius wider than 20 miles, as this dilutes your local authority in Google's database.
  • Address Matching: Ensure the address displayed on your profile matches the exact address listed on your corporate registration, business licenses, and utilities.

Pillar 3: Prominence

Prominence reflects how well-known, established, and authoritative your business is in the digital space. If two profiles are equally close and relevant, Google will rank the business with higher prominence first.

How to Optimize Prominence:

  • Local Citations & Directory Links: Build matches of your Business Name, Address, and Phone Number (NAP) on authoritative directories such as Apple Maps, Bing Places, Yelp, and local chamber of commerce portals. Consistency is key.
  • Review Velocity and Ratings: Google values the frequency, volume, and quality of customer ratings. A business that receives three 5-star reviews every week has higher prominence than a business that hasn't received a review in six months.
  • User Interaction Signals: The number of times users click "Call", request maps directions, or click through to your website tells Google your profile is popular and active, boosting your prominence score.

Summary Checklist for Profile Growth

To start improving your rankings immediately, execute these three organic actions:

  1. Log in to your merchant portal and fill out every single missing information field (hours, accessibility, attributes).
  2. Take three high-resolution photos of your storefront or team weekly and upload them directly to your listing.
  3. Use SMS templates or QR cards to ask every satisfied customer to leave feedback on your Google Business Profile.
